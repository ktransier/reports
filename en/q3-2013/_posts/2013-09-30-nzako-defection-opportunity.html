Mass LRA Defection Opportunity in Nzako, CAR?

In August 2013, LRA forces near Banale, CAR made contact with local residents, delivering letters addressed to the local authorities in nearby Nzako and to Michael Djotodia, head of the Transitional Authorities in CAR. The letters, reportedly sent at the request of LRA leader Joseph Kony, indicated that a large group of LRA members were interested in defecting and possibly settling in Central African territory. They were delivered by a group of LRA representatives from a group operating in the area under the command of Major Otto "Sam" Ladeere, known as a trusted lieutenant of Kony's who has previously served as both his bodyguard and the LRA's Director of Intelligence. It has reportedly told local residents that 1,000-2,000 LRA members are interested in defecting, though this number is undoubtedly an estimate. Experts place the total size of the LRA between 500-600 members, and many of these groups are operating in other areas of CAR and Congo.

Banale and Nzako are in an area encompassing parts of Haut Kotto and Mbomou prefectures that is rich in diamonds, gold, and uranium.
