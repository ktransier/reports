I. LRA targets Central African towns despite Ugandan, US military presence

The surge in LRA activity in CAR's Haut Mbomou prefecture is unprecedented in recent years, with attack and abduction totals in Q1 2014 nearing or surpassing yearly totals in both 2012 and 2013. The attacks were also unusually bold, with many targeting vehicles and communities near Ugandan and US military bases. Nearly two-thirds of the people abducted in these attacks were forced to porter looted goods into the bush before being released by their captors. During at least 4 attacks, LRA forces asked abductees detailed questions about the location of military forces and nearby communities, as well as questions on the movement of civilians along roads.

The general increase in LRA activity in the region combined with this renewed resolve against security forces could suggest a certain level of desperation from LRA members, who are experiencing a severe lack of resources as a result of the dry season and increased pressure of counter-LRA forces, which are keeping them on the move. This trend in LRA attacks seems to suggest that LRA forces in Haut-Mbomou are re-supplying themselves during the dry season through highly targeted lootings. Overall, this pattern of LRA activity sustains the idea that the LRA forces’ main goal remains survival.
